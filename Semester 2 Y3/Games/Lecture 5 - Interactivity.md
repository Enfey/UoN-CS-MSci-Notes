# Systematic Game Design
- **First-Order design problem**
	- Functional consequences of design
	- What the system does
	- Rules and mechanics
- **Second-Order design problem**
	- Psycho-social consequences of design
	- How players *feel*
	- Cultural meaning 
	- Emotional impact
- Thus, the designer creates the formal system, the rules, and the explicit interactions within the system.
- The experiential and cultural systems are emergent from this formal system.
- The designer indirectly designs the player's experience by directly designing the formal system and its rules and interactions. 

## Design Counterparts/MDA framework
- **MDA** - A Formal Approach To Game Design and Research

- **Mechanics**
	- Static component of the game
	- Comprised of data structures, static rules, algorithms
		- Damage formula
		- Jump height
- **Dynamics**
	- Run-time behaviour of mechanics and rules acting on player inputs, and subsequent outputs.
	- Game loops, patterns of play that emerge from the mechanics 'in the wild'
	- Facilitate creative problem-solving, unintended tactics, difficulty balancing
		- Mechanics produce dynamics like comebacks, snowballing, stalemates. 
- **Aesthetics**
	- Desirable emotional responses invoked in the player
	- Cognitive interactivity etc

### Understanding Player Experience
- A multi-valent model of interactivity
	- From usability to user experience - the experience of use
	- From game playability to player experience - the experience of play
- While related, they are not the same. We introduce fundamental kinds of interactivity
	- **Designed**
		- **Functional Interactivity**
			- Responsivess
			- Clear controls/interface
			- Feedback
			- *Usability*
		- **Explicit interactivity**
			- Participation with designed choices and procedures e.g., using WASD to move character in destiny
			- Choices, random events, rules and procedures
			- Defining what we mean when we say that games are interactive
				- Allowing the player to make choices within that system
	- **Emergent**
		- **Cognitive Interactivity**
			- Interpretation
			- Imagination
			- Psychological, emotional, intellectual interaction between player and system
		- **Cultural Interactivity**
			- Emergent, participation within the culture of the object
			- Fan-culture
				- Memes, videos, cosplays

## Heuristic Evaluation
- Heuristics are usability principles
	- E.g., Nielsen: The system should always keep users informed about what is going on, through appropriate feedback within reasonable time
- Hochleitner extends this to games
- Identify usability problems in line with heuristics during early stages of design, prior to user-based testing, determine how well the game scores when evaluated using these heuristics
- Game specific heuristics ton be used in evaluation:
	- **Learning**
		- The player is given space to make mistakes, but the failure conditions must be understandable
	- **Control**
		- The player feels they are in control; that includes control over the character, but also the impact onto the game world
		- It is clear what is happening in the game. 
	- **Feedback**
		- The feedback is given immediately to the player's action
		- The player does not have to memorise resources like bullets, life, score, points, and ammunition
	- **Interaction**
		- The first player action is obvious and results in immediate positive feedback
	- **Motivation**
		- The game and the outcome are perceived as being fair.

### Self-Determination Theory
- Games are primarily self/intrinsically motivating
	- Vs external motivations such as money, points
	- Satisfaction of basic psychological needs predicts enjoyment, playing persistance, improved wellbeing
- 3 psychological needs that are behind intrinsic motivation
	1.  **Competence**
		- Games channel a sense of competence and mastery
		- Via providing motivational feedback and challenges
	2. **Autonomy**
		- Actions are self-endorsed and performed willingly
		- Choice and volition
	3. **Relatedness**
		- Empathy with characters, a sense of belonging. 
- Measured by player experience of need satisfaction (PENS)
